Research

Exploring exoplanets and stellar variability through advanced data analysis

Current Research

Exoplanet Characterization

Imperial College London & Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile

As a Fulbright Research Fellow, I'm developing advanced methods for characterizing exoplanets by studying their atmospheric properties. My research focuses on reducing the impact of stellar variability on atmospheric studies using sophisticated stellar models.

Exoplanets Atmospheric Analysis Stellar Models

Stellar Variability & Light Curve Analysis

Imperial College London

Investigating stellar variability patterns and their effects on exoplanet detection and characterization. Using Gaussian Process models and advanced statistical techniques to separate stellar signals from planetary signatures.

Stellar Variability Light Curves Gaussian Processes

Previous Research

Exoplanet Detectability Study

University of Florida

Led a comprehensive research study on exoplanet detectability using transit photometry. Developed and implemented transit injection and recovery pipelines to test detection algorithms under various stellar conditions.

Transit Photometry Detection Algorithms Pipeline Development

Gravitational Wave Counterparts

University of Michigan - Dark Energy Survey

Contributed to the Dark Energy Survey collaboration, focusing on identifying and analyzing optical counterparts to gravitational wave events. Worked on developing automated detection and classification systems.

Gravitational Waves Optical Counterparts Dark Energy Survey

Technosignature Search

University of California, Berkeley

Searched for technosignatures—potential signs of intelligent extraterrestrial life—around nearby stars. Analyzed radio and optical data for anomalous signals that could indicate advanced civilizations.
